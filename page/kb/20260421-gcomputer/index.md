## TL;DR
The [Edge 850](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/p/1630197/pn/010-03023-00/) is the better buy if you want Garmin’s newest computer with a better screen, speaker, storage, and navigation/training features; but the [Edge 840](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/p/798777/pn/010-02695-00/) is the smarter value for most riders because it delivers the core experience for less. The bigger Garmin models make sense mainly for multi-day rides, riders who struggle to read navigation on smaller screens, or people who want a larger display even if that means mount fit issues. The solar option is not worth prioritizing because it does not  deliver meaningful improvement.<br>

## Garmin 840 vs 850: What changed?
The Edge 850 uses a slightly larger 2.7-inch display with higher resolution, while the 840 has a 2.6-inch, lower-resolution screen.
* The Edge 850 adds a built-in speaker for audible prompts and an electronic bike bell; the Edge 840 does not.
* The Edge 850 doubles internal storage to 64 GB, which is useful if you like having more maps and data on-device.
* Garmin also adds newer features on the 850 such as Garmin Pay, 5 Hz GPS recording, Smart Fueling, and expanded Garmin Cycle Coach tools.
* The Edge 840 still covers the essentials well: touchscreen plus buttons, strong navigation, workout support, and Garmin’s core cycling ecosystem

## Target Market 
* Choose the Edge 840 if you want a proven, well-rounded bike computer and care more about value than having Garmin’s newest extras. Reviews consistently frame it as the better everyday pick unless you specifically want the 850’s display and speaker upgrades.
* Choose the Edge 850 if you routinely depend on on-device navigation, want a brighter and easier-to-read screen, or like the convenience of audible cues and Garmin Pay. It is the more premium and more refined option, not the more economical one.
* Look at the larger Garmin computers only if you do multi-day rides, need a bigger screen because small navigation displays are hard to read, or want a unit that is easier to follow at a glance even though it may be bulkier and less friendly to many mounts.
* Skip the solar version unless you have a very specific use case, because it does not offer a substantial enough improvement to justify choosing it over the standard model.

## Practical takeaway
For most riders, the Edge 840 is the sweet spot: it **saves you about $100** gives you Garmin’s core navigation and training features without the extra cost of the 850. The Edge 850 is the nicer device, but it is an upgrade of convenience and polish rather than a transformational leap. If your rides are long enough that screen size and battery matter more than portability, that is when stepping up to Garmin’s larger units starts to make sense.
