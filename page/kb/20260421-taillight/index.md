## Garmin Varia 820 vs. Varia 515: What’s Different?
Garmin’s new Varia [RearVue 820](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/p/1689121/) is a meaningful step up from the older [Varia RTL515](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/p/698001/), but it is not a drop-in replacement. The 820 adds advanced radar awareness, brighter lighting, longer battery life, USB-C charging, and tracking features that make it feel modern to the 515. At the same time, those improvements require an newer bicycle computer, so factor in the requirements before upgrading.

## Brief differences
* More advanced radar detection: The 820 detects vehicles farther, with a wider field of view.
* Better vehicle detail: The 820 can identify vehicle size and threat level more intelligently
* Brighter rear light: The 820’s light is more visible and adds a brake-light function, which the 515 does not offer in the same way.
* Longer battery life: The 820 lasts longer, especially in flashing modes for longer rides.
* USB-C charging: The 820 moves to USB-C, while the 515 uses micro-USB.
* Newer feature set: The 820 includes same-speed vehicle tracking, a common complaint with older models.
* Heavier and pricier: The 820 costs and weighs more, so the upgrade is not just “the same but newer.”

## Why the 820 makes sense
For riders who spend time in traffic, the 820 is the compelling option because it gives information sooner, with better precision. That extra clarity matters when you are trying to judge whether a car is approaching quickly, closing slowly, or **sitting in your draft.** If you want the most capable rear radar Garmin currently offers, the 820 is the one to push toward.

## Computer compatibility matters
The biggest catch is that the 820’s advanced features are not equally useful on every bike computer. Some of its best functions are designed for newer Garmin head units or can be reduced when paired with older displays or simpler devices. In practical terms, a rider may need to budget for a new bicycle computer (X30 series and above) to get the full value out of the 820.

## Practical recommendation for either model
If the goal is maximum rear awareness and a more premium safety setup, the Varia 820 is the better buy over the 515, especially for riders who already use a modern compatible computer. If the goal is the simplest and least expensive radar solution, the 515 still does the job, saves about $100 and remains a solid basic option.

## Final thoughts: Consider the camera
That said, a camera is often the safest and most practical choice overall. A camera adds evidence, not just awareness, and that can matter in an accident, a close pass, or a road-rage incident. It costs more up front, but it can easily pay for itself in the event of a citation or injury.  I always recommend the [Varia 715](https://www.garmin.com/en-US/p/721258/) if you can afford it.
