## New Mechanic On-board Checklist

Finding the right mix of competency, work style and personality for a bicycle shop is notoriously difficult.  This checklist focuses on protecting our shop's reputation, maintaining safety, and getting them efficient on day one.

### Operational & System Integration
* **POS & Work Order Training:** Walk through how our shop logs service writing, tracks parts allocation, and updates customers (especially with automated SMS & email updates).
* **Inventory & Parts Sourcing:** Show them the layout of small parts (cables, housing, brake pads, common bottom brackets) and the protocol for pulling inventory vs. special-ordering.
* **Tool Stewardship & Station Setup:** Define expectations for tool cleaning, shadows board management, and communal tool usage (e.g., specific bearing press kits or wheel truing stands).

### Safety & Risk Management
* **Chemical & Fluid Disposal:** Review local regulations and shop layout for disposing of DOT fluid, mineral oil, degreasers, and soiled rags.
* **Heavy Lifting & Ergonomics:** Review protocols for handling heavy builds, lifting heavy cargo bikes or e-bikes onto repair stands, and proper use of hoist systems.
* **Battery Safety:** Walk through safe handling, charging protocols, and the designated storage or quarantine zones for compromised or damaged batteries.

### Culture & Quality Assurance
* **The "Double Check" Protocol:** Establish who inspects a bike before it is marked complete (e.g., a service manager signature on high-liability work like brake bleeds or drivetrain overhauls).
* **Customer Communication Baseline:** Define how the mechanic handles unexpected discoveries during a teardown (e.g., documenting a hairline frame crack or an unquoted worn chainring) before contacting the client.

## 10 Technical Competency Challenges

To verify their skill level and attention to detail, use these 10 practical bench challenges. Observe not just their speed, but their cleanliness, tool selection, and torque discipline.

### Hydraulic Brake Bleed & Pad Assessment: *Time limit: 20 mins*
Have them perform a full bleed on a spongy hydraulic brake system (one Shimano mineral oil, one SRAM DOT fluid). <br>
*What to look for:* Correct fluid isolation, crisp lever feel, clean cleanup to prevent pad contamination, and proper torque on bleed ports.

### Internal Cable Routing Routing & Cockpit Setup: *Time limit: 30 mins*
Provide a modern carbon frame with fully internal routing through the headset. Have them route a fresh shift housing or brake hose.<br>
*What to look for:* Use of routing tools, avoiding internal frame rattles, appropriate housing length for bar rotation, and clean cockpit management.

### Tubeless Conversion & Tire Seating: *Time limit: 15 mins*
Give them a dry rim and tire to set up tubeless.<br>
*What to look for:* Precise rim taping technique (no bubbles, correct overlap), valve nut tightness, seating the bead cleanly without damaging the tire, and appropriate sealant volume.

### Wheel Truing & Tension Balancing: *Time limit: 20 mins*
Hand them a wheel with a noticeable lateral wobble and uneven tension.<br>
*What to look for:* Use of a spoke tensiometer to verify uniform tension rather than just chasing the wobble, checking for radial true, and dish accuracy.

### Electronic Drivetrain Diagnostics & Firmware: *Time limit: 15 mins*
Present a bike with a mis-shifting electronic drivetrain (SRAM AXS or Shimano Di2). <br>
*What to look for:* Checking micro-adjustments, checking battery health, verifying limit screws, and navigating the respective app to check firmware status or error logs.

### Bottom Bracket Diagnostic & Installation: *Time limit: 20 mins*
Provide a bike with a mysterious bottom bracket creak. <br>
*What to look for:* Methodical troubleshooting (checking chainring bolts and pedals first), correct extraction tool selection (especially for PressFit), cleaning the shell, and using the correct grease, anti-seize, or retaining compound on re-installation.

### Dropper Post Overhaul or Cable Replacement: *Time limit: 25 mins*
Have them service a sticky dropper post or replace an internally routed actuator cable. <br>
*What to look for:* Managing cable tension at the remote, correct torque on the seatpost collar (to prevent binding the internal mechanism), and structural grease application.

### Suspension Air Sleeve Service: *Time limit: 30 mins*
Perform a basic 50-hour air sleeve service on a common rear shock or lower leg fork service.<br>
*What to look for:* Strict cleanliness to avoid introducing grit, careful removal of old seals without scratching metal surfaces, correct oil/grease volumes, and proper pressure resetting.

### Derailleur Hanger Alignment & Mechanical Tuning: *Time limit: 15 mins*
Intentionally bend a rear derailleur hanger slightly out of alignment.<br>
*What to look for:* Immediate deployment of a hanger alignment tool before touching derailleur limit screws, checking alignment across multiple points on the wheel, and crisp mechanical indexing afterward.

### The 5-Minute Safety & Precision Audit: *Time limit: 5 mins*
Hand them a completed bike ready for customer pickup, into which you have subtly introduced 3 subtle errors (e.g., a backwards front tire tread, a slightly loose stem bolt, or a misaligned brake caliper).<br>
*What to look for:* Their systematic approach to a pre-delivery inspection and whether they catch all hidden liabilities.

**Pro Tip:** When performing these challenges, the best mechanics aren't always the fastest. Observe ones who naturally reach for a calibrated torque wrench for every critical fastener and leave their workstation cleaner than they found it.

## Competency Grading


To accurately assess a bicycle mechanic's competency, style, and efficiency, you cannot rely on a verbal interview alone. <br>
A hands-on bench test is essential.

**Level 1:** Entry-Level / Junior Mechanic Assessment <br>
**Level 2:** Mid-Level / Experienced Mechanic Assessment <br>

A scoring rubric to grade acceptance objectively will vary between candidate experience, certifications, and riding discipline.
