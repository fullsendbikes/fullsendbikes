## Mid-Week Spin: The Redwood Shores Gravel Loop 🚴‍♂️💨

Looking to shake off the workday gridlock and swap screen time for some shoreline singletrack/hardpack? Grab your bike, because we've got the ultimate mid-week, after-work escape dialed in. This route takes full advantage of the gorgeous San Francisco Bay Trail looping entirely around the Redwood Shores peninsula.  Do 1 lap for fun, 2 laps for exercise, 1 lap for a FABULOUS night ride experience!

[Strava Map](https://www.strava.com/routes/3499072574506358160) or [Garmin Course](https://connect.garmin.com/app/course/472867753) maps out an elegant, flat, and lightning-fast 8.24-mile loop with virtually zero climbing (a gentle 54.7 feet of cumulative rollers over the whole ride). It is a pure, scenic cruise that splits its time beautifully between smooth pavement and fast-rolling levee gravel.

## ⏱️ Ride Time & Sunset Strategy

* Wheels Rolling: 6:00 PM
* Sunset: ~8:30 PM
* Expected Ride Time: 35 to 45 minutes (for a non-beginner cyclist maintaining a steady 11–14 mph pace).

Because this snappy loop leaves you with plenty of daylight to spare, it’s the perfect route to do a double lap if you want to push it past 16 miles, or simply kick back at the viewpoints without ever having to panic about racing the sun.

## 📍 Where to Meet & What to Expect

**The Launchpad:** Kick things off from the Oracle Parkway parking lot (right off Marine Parkway). It’s easily accessible, has plenty of space to prep your gear, and connects seamlessly to the trail.  Or [Shores KinderCare](https://maps.app.goo.gl/C7b4BARyvW6iDhxa6) for a South Bay Friendly parking spot.

**The Experience:** Expect wide-open skies, refreshing bay breezes, and a fantastic break from vehicle traffic. The route feels incredibly remote despite being right in our tech-heavy backyard. You’ll wind along wide concrete pathways, trace residential lagoons, and power down long, uninterrupted stretches of graded levee gravel.

Keep an eye out for a couple of tight 90-degree corners when transitioning on and off the levee embankments—keep your weight centered and look through the turns.

### 🗺️ Points of Interest

* **Belmont Slough & Bird Island:** Early in the ride, stop by the trailside viewing stations. Use the tower viewers to scan for snowy egrets, blue herons, and avocets wading out near Bird Island.  Coyotes have been spotted along this trail, too.

* **The Historic Radio Station (Radio Road):** Keep your eyes peeled for the historic 1941 radio transmitter building near the tip of the peninsula, which played a vital national security role during WWII.

* **Steinberger Slough & Bair Island Views:** The eastern leg of the gravel levee offers stunning, panoramic views of the bright blue water and the massive wetland restoration projects over on Middle and Outer Bair Island.

## 🌤️ How to Dress: The "Warm Start, Cool Finish" Kit

Starting at 6:00 PM on a warm afternoon can be deceptive. The sun will still be warm, but as it dips lower and the Bay winds kick up, the temperature will drop rapidly.

* **The Base:** Go with a lightweight, breathable short-sleeve jersey and your favorite bib shorts.

* **The Essential Add-on:** Stash a windproof vest (gillet) or a lightweight windbreaker in your jersey pocket. You’ll be grateful for that extra layer around 6:45 PM when you're hammering down the exposed levee paths into a coastal headwind.

* **Eyesight:** A pair of cycling glasses with photochromic (transition) or clear lenses are a game-changer here to protect your eyes from evening midges and dust as the light fades.

## 🔧 Bike Setup: Tires & PSI

While a pure road or mountain bike *can* push through, a dedicated gravel rig, cross bike, or an all-road setup is where the fun is truly at.


## 🛞 Tire Recommendation

Opt for a semi-slick or fast-rolling file-tread gravel tire (such as a Maxxis Receptor, Panaracer GravelKing SS, or Schwalbe G-One Speed) in a width of 32mm to 40mm. You don't need aggressive knobs here—smooth center treads will fly on the paved segments, while the side files will give you all the confidence you need on the hardpack and loose dust.  

A hardtail mountain bike with more narrow (29x2.1) treads work well, too.

### 💨 Pressure Guide (Targeting 35mm–38mm Tubeless Tires)

To keep your rolling resistance low on the pavement without skittering across the gravel corners, dial in your PSI based on your riding weight (rider + bike + gear):

| Riding Weight | Front PSI | Rear PSI 
|--- |--- |--- 
| Lightweight (< 150 lbs) | 26–28 PSI | 28–30 PSI 
| Average (150–180 lbs) | 30–32 PSI | 32–34 PSI 
| Heavyweight (180+ lbs) | 34–36 PSI | 36–38 PSI 

*(Note: If you are running traditional inner tubes instead of tubeless, bump these numbers up by 5–7 PSI to prevent pinch flats on the gravel transitions)*
