## Ride summary for newer road cyclists

This is a 16-mile out-and-back road ride called San Bruno Park Climb, starting near the South San Francisco Caltrain station. <br>
* ​Distance: 16.0 miles.
* ​Elevation: about 1,400 ft of total climbing, concentrated in one main hill rather than constant steep rollers.
* ​Effort level: Good for newer riders who can already ride 10–15 miles; the hill will feel like a solid but doable challenge, with flatter and downhill sections to warm up and cool down.
* Time: Expect about 1.5–2 hours of riding time for a newer road cyclist, not counting a longer coffee stop. <br> 

Think of it as: easy spin out of town → one main climb with views → relaxed roll back.

Take Caltrain to the starting point, or park for free along the Bayside Trail near The [Cove at Oyster Point.](http://www.covessf.com/). Plenty of restroom stops, public bike repair stations, hikers, and park rangers nearby so you're never "too far."

### Description, views, and points of interest

* **Bay and shoreline views:** The route heads toward the Bay past Point San Bruno Park, a small shoreline park with picnic tables, a rock jetty, and big views north toward Oyster Point and San Francisco, and south toward SFO and the airport traffic.
* **“Hidden” park in the biotech zone:** Point San Bruno Park feels like a tucked‑away green space surrounded by South San Francisco’s biotech and office buildings, which makes riding there feel like discovering a quiet spot in an industrial area.
* **Hillside scenery:** As you climb away from the shoreline area toward San Bruno Park/Mountain, the backdrop shifts from water and warehouses to open hillsides, radio towers, and natural open space on San Bruno Mountain, giving you that “out of town” feeling without going far.
* **Coffee at Gateway Blvd:** Around Gateway Blvd you have cafes like Baysider Cafe (601 Gateway Blvd) that are perfect for a mid‑ride or return‑leg coffee and snack in a landscaped, campus‑style setting.
​
You can describe this ride to others as: Caltrain → Bay edge and quiet park with planes overhead → hillside climb with views → coffee at Gateway → easy roll back.​

## Parking notes

* **Caltrain station lot:** Use the South San Francisco Caltrain station parking; Caltrain runs and maintains paid parking at its stations, and SSF’s lot has been recently improved (better lighting, drainage, and surfaces), making it a practical and straightforward place to start and finish your ride.
* **Easy rollout:** From the lot, you can roll directly onto nearby streets to pick up your route without complicated navigation.
* **Transit option:** If anyone in your group prefers not to drive, they can also ride Caltrain to the SSF station and join the ride from there.
* **BART Consideration:** San Bruno BART is 2.5 miles (one way) and adds 14 minutes of additional riding.

**Distance:** 16.0 miles <br>
**Estimated ride time:** For very newer riders on road bikes, 1 hour 45 minutes to 2 hours.  Fit riders will complete the course in 1 hour 20 minutes to 1 hour 45 minutes.<br>
