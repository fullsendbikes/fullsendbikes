## "Beautiful Mountain" - Two Route Options
Monte Bello [Open Space Preserve](https://www.openspace.org/preserves/monte-bello) is a jewel of the Santa Cruz Mountains, offering a mix of rugged canyon descents and ridge-top views. Easily accessed from the SF Peninsula, the park provides a classic Northern California riding experience with rolling golden grasslands and deep, shaded forests.  Add "Adobe Creek Trail" (aka "White Oak Trail") for the best singletrack experience the park offers.  

## Ride Details
1. **The Singletrack Warm-up:** From the Page Mill lot, take White Oak Trail. This is a narrow, playful descent through dense woodland. It features some off-camber sections and tight switchbacks that are perfect for an intermediate rider to find their flow. <br>
1. **The Connector:** At the bottom, follow Skid Road Trail (an old logging path) which connects you briefly to the Stevens Creek Nature Trail. <br>
1. **The Grunt:** You’ll merge onto Canyon Trail for a steady climb. Be prepared—there is a notoriously steep "wall" section on Canyon that hits a 25% grade. It's short but will definitely spike your heart rate. <br>
1. **The High Point:** Transition to Bella Vista Trail. This is a stunning, steady climb (roughly 7-10% grade) with no tree cover, providing massive views of the Santa Clara Valley. <br>
1. **The Summit:** Follow Indian Creek to Monte Bello Road to reach the Black Mountain Summit. <br>
1. **The Return:** Retrace your steps back down Bella Vista (it's a fast, fun descent in this direction) and finish via the upper section of Canyon Trail back to the parking lot. <br>

## Amenities
**Parking:** Sizable main parking on Page Mill Road. <br>
**Facilities:** Restrooms and trail map kiosk in parking area (no drinking fountains) <br>
**Views:** ride directly over the San Andreas Fault. Look for "sag ponds"—natural depressions formed by fault activity—near the Canyon Trail. <br>
**Seasonal Closures:** Note that White Oak Trail is often closed during the rainy season

## What to Bring
**Windbreaker:** Even if it’s 80°F in Palo Alto, the wind at the Black Mountain summit can be piercing. <br>
**Bug Spray:** Because this route spends more time in the deep canopy of White Oak and Skid Road, mosquitoes can be a nuisance if you stop for too long in the shaded sections during spring and summer. <br>
