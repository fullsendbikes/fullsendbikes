## Garbage to Goldmine
This hidden gem for local riders and Meta employees. [Located at the edge](https://maps.google.com/?q=%201600%20Marsh%20Road%20%20Menlo%20Park,%20CA%2094025) of the Don Edwards San Francisco Bay National Wildlife Refuge, it offers a unique mix of salt marsh scenery and punchy, artificial hills that provide a surprisingly good workout for being in the middle of a flat marshland.  Bring a gravel bike for the hardpack and pea gravel, or a hardtail MTB for the steepest interval training.

## The Nitty-Gritty
**Intervals:** avoid flat perimeter and head to inner trails <br>
**Elevation:** These hills are short but steep, offering interval training. <br>

## Park History & Context 
The park’s rolling hills are not natural; Bedwell is a reclaimed sanitary landfill. <br>
The Transformation: Originally established in 1957 as the Marsh Road Landfill, it took in waste until 1984. <br>
From Trash to Trails: In the mid-80s, the site was capped with clay and soil to create 160 acres of passive recreation space. <br>
Eco-Legacy: Today, it serves as a critical habitat for migratory birds and features the Great Spirit Path, an art installation of 53 rock sculptures that double as a "stone poem" (pictured above).<br>

## Amenities
**Parking:** Two paved lots (free) are available at the end of the entrance road.
**Facilities:** Restrooms and drinking fountains are located near the main parking area.
**Terrain:** A 2.3-mile flat perimeter loop (gravel/paved) and an interior web of steep, unpaved trails.
**Views:** 360-degree views of the Dumbarton Bridge, South Bay salt ponds, and the Santa Cruz Mountains.

## What to Bring
**Windbreaker:** It is almost always windy on the bay; even a warm day can feel chilly once you stop moving.
**Tire Pressure:** If on a gravel bike, drop your pressure slightly; the interior trails can be "kitty litter" (loose gravel over hardpack) in the summer.
**Water:** There is no shade on the trails, so even a quick lunch ride requires hydration.
